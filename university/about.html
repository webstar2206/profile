About Us

Banasthali Vidyapith aims at the synthesis of spiritual values and scientific achievements of both the East and the West. Its educational programme is based on the concept of "Panchmukhi Shiksha" and aims at all round harmonious development of personality. Emphasis on Indian culture and thought characterized by simple living and khadi wearing are hallmarks of life at Banasthali.

Banasthali Vidyapith stands out as a fully residential institute exclusively for women's higher education, providing a comprehensive educational continuum from primary to Ph.D. levels. Founded on October 6, 1935, by Smt. Ratan Shastri and Pandit Hiralal Shastri, Banasthali emerged from the tragic loss of their daughter Shantabai, reflecting a phoenix-like rebirth from sorrow.

As one of India's five premier higher education institutions for women, Banasthali has evolved over seventy-five years into a national epicenter for women's education. Its educational ethos centers on holistic student development, epitomized by the Five-fold Educational Programme (Panchmukhi Shiksha), integrating physical, practical, aesthetic, moral, and intellectual dimensions.

Nestled in a rural setting, Banasthali fosters simplicity, self-reliance, and tolerance, facilitating the integration of personality development. Embracing Indian heritage, the institution empowers women for leadership roles.

Leveraging its autonomy as a deemed university, Banasthali has innovatively structured its curriculum, spanning traditional and emerging subjects up to doctoral research.
